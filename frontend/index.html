What is the minimum pension amount guaranteed under the Employees' Pension Scheme 1995, and who is eligible to receive it?
Today, 14:32

Under the Employees' Pension Scheme (EPS) 1995, the Government of India guarantees a minimum monthly pension of ₹1,000 per month to eligible members.


Eligibility criteria

  • The member must have completed 10 years of contributory service.
  • The member must have attained the age of 58 years (superannuation) or 50 years (early pension, reduced rate).
  • Pension is payable on permanent and total disablement regardless of service period.
  • Nominee/family members are eligible for widow, children, and orphan pension upon the member's death.

The ₹1,000 minimum was introduced via the EPS Amendment 2014 and is funded partly by the Central Government contribution of 1.16% of wages.

Show me the contribution rate tables for EPF 1952 — both employee and employer shares.
Today, 14:35

Under the Employees' Provident Funds Scheme 1952, contributions are split between the employee and employer as follows:


Standard Establishment (20+ employees)

  • Employee: 12% of basic wages + dearness allowance + retaining allowance
  • Employer: 12% split as 3.67% to EPF + 8.33% to EPS (capped at ₹15,000 wage ceiling)
  • Employer admin charges: 0.50% to EPF admin + 0.50% to EDLI
